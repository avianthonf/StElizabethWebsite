# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective families are the primary audience: parents evaluating whether St. Elizabeth High School is the right environment for their child, and students forming an early emotional impression of the school’s character, ambition, and care. They are likely arriving with limited context, comparing multiple schools, and looking for both trust signals and a sense of belonging.

Secondary audiences include the current school community, but the default experience should be optimized first for persuasion, clarity, and confidence-building for new visitors.

## Product Purpose

This site exists to present St. Elizabeth High School as a compelling, credible, and distinctive choice for secondary education in Goa. It should translate the school’s Catholic identity, academic seriousness, and community spirit into a modern digital experience that feels memorable rather than merely informational.

Success looks like prospective families quickly understanding what the school stands for, feeling that it offers a serious and nurturing environment, and being motivated to explore further or begin the admissions process.

## Brand Personality

Warm, dignified, aspirational.

The voice should feel welcoming and human, but never casual or flimsy. It should communicate care, discipline, and ambition without sounding corporate, promotional, or ornamental for its own sake. The emotional goal is to create trust first, then admiration, then momentum.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a corporate dashboard or a software product interface. Avoid flat utilitarian layouts, overly functional UI metaphors, sterile panel-based compositions, or interactions that feel operational instead of invitational.

It should also avoid generic institutional sameness, where the site feels like a stock school template with interchangeable copy and predictable section patterns.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with identity, not administration. The experience should foreground the school’s character, values, and atmosphere before operational details.
2. Show seriousness through craft. Academic credibility and institutional confidence should come through visual precision, clear structure, and disciplined motion, not through stiffness.
3. Build emotional trust quickly. Every major surface should help prospective families feel both welcomed and reassured that the school is capable, grounded, and intentional.
4. Make aspiration feel attainable. The site should feel elevated and memorable while still accessible to ordinary families, never exclusive or luxury-coded.
5. Use motion as meaning. Animation should deepen narrative, focus attention, and create presence, while remaining optional for visitors who prefer reduced motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default target: WCAG 2.1 AA.

The site should support keyboard navigation, clear semantic structure, strong color contrast, and screen-reader legibility across all primary experiences. Motion should honor reduced-motion preferences without breaking comprehension or narrative flow. Visual sophistication must not come at the expense of readability, orientation, or input comfort.
